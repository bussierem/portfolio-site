Résumé
RPGs I was introduced to Tabletop RPGs right before college in 2007 by some friends. They introduced me to Pathfinder, and I have been playing various RPGs consistently since then, with a wide variety of systems:
Medieval: D&D (3e, 3.5e, 4e, 5e), Pathfinder, Shadows of the Demon Lord, 13th Age, Iron Heroes
Cyberpunk: Shadowrun (4e, 5e), Cyberpunk v3
Warhammer 40k: Rogue Trader, Dark Heresy 2e, Only War, Freebootaz
World of Darkness: Hunter: The Vigil, Mage: The Awakening
Rules Light/One-shots: Everyone is John, Lasers and Feelings, All Outta Bubblegum, FATE, Dungeon World, Mythender
Misc: Monsters and Other Childish Things, Numenera, Feng Shui 2e, Savage Worlds (Deadlands)
Board Games I have played dozens of board games (I won't list these out) of all kinds, from the highly popular ones such as Ticket To Ride and Settlers of Catan, to more obscure ones like Epic Resort and Hirelings. Playing all of these games has given me experience examining the systems of a game and finding problems, breaking points, and well-designed rules.
Dungeon Master I have been DMing Tabletop RPGs since 2008 - Right after learning to play, I immediately went to college and found a group, where I began my DM career. Since then I have DMed probably 90% of the systems I have played (listed above), and been able to DM in many different situations: Online games, in-person games, games with adults, games with kids (I taught five 12y/o kids how to play Dungeon World, and it was a blast).
Out of all the games I have DMed, I would say maybe 40% have gone to a point I would call "campaign end", due to various reasons (the nebulous nature of online games being a primary factor).
Additionally, every game I have DMed for has had at least one person that was completely new to RPGs, and I have had the pleasure of teaching each one how to play, which has been a valuable experience itself. Finally, I have also created a number of custom worlds. My style of DMing leans more towards "minimal prep improvisation", which allows me to make very fun adventures for my party on the fly, crafting enemies, quests, NPCs, and rewards whenever needed.
RPG Design During all my time playing different RPG systems, it has given me many chances to compare, contrast, and analyze the rulesets of these systems. Doing so piqued my interest into making my own, and I have had numerous chances at varying levels of success (see Rulesets for an example of this).
Additionally, I have had a chance to deconstruct and try to "break" systems, with the help of my friend Tyler, allowing us to find the breakpoints in rules and even implement our own homebrew rules that "fix" things we find to be lacking.
Software Development My full-time career is as a software developer and tester. I believe that this primary passion for programming has given me an analytical view, allowing me to deconstruct systems and truly enjoy learning and applying these rules.
Also, this career has given me the ability to make several tools and apps to be used in my RPGs - dice rollers, map generation, character creators, etc.
Grammar An odd thing to have on a résumé, but here we are. I pride myself on my spelling, grammar, and editing abilities - I have always been excellent at this, and I carry it over to whatever I work on.
